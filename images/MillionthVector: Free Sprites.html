    > As per your request I have sharpened and re-uploaded the big faction 6 spaceship, odd, it did seem a bit blurrier than the rest.

    Hmm... No, you have to resave it from the original source (3D model?). Now one can see that it's sharpened - it's not better, the flaw is just a different one now. Compare the ship to the others, you clearly see the sharpening on all lines. :)

    > As for credit, just put a mention of MillionthVector somewhere.

    Yeah, well... You know, the problem is that people who use your free sprites can't feel save (from lawyers) because you as the creator can always withdraw your permission any time you want. That's not possible when you release your free stuff under a free license as the license at release or when one downloaded your stuff counts. And you're as safe with it as without - it's just better for people who use it. Of course if you want it like that, that's your right. It just makes it very unsafe to use your stuff, but if that's your wish, then it's ok. Also, you don't write if one is allowed to redistribute modified versions of your ships (while still crediting you)... You know it's all complicated and unsave without a license.

    That's why i recommended Creative Commons. It is in fact Copyright, but you as a creator have much more freedom to choose and decide what is allowed and what now. Plus it gives people safety. Here for example, the CC BY-SA license: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/ (SA = ShareAlike, it means that people must release modifications under the same or a almost identical license, you can even combine that with NC, which prohibits a commercial use and other stuff). The sense of CC is to make Copyright easy (readable for non lawyers) while being save. It's widely used on the internet and offline by all kinds of artists! :)
